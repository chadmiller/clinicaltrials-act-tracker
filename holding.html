FDAAA Compliance Tracker

The best currently available evidence shows that the results of clinical trials are routinely withheld from doctors, researchers, and patients. This is a global public health scandal.

The FDA Amendment Act of 2007 (FDAAA) requires certain clinical trials to report their results on ClinicalTrials.gov. Despite extensive evidence of breaches, no fines have ever been issued.

The Final Rule to properly implement this law has now finally come into force. The first trials required to report results under the new FDAAA rules will begin to come due on January 18, 2018. There is then a one month delay to results being posted.

On February 16, 2018 this website will go live, showing which individual sponsors and trials have breached their legal requirement to report results.

The FDAAA Compliance Tracker is built by Seb Bacon, Nick DeVito, and Ben Goldacre at the EBM DataLab in the University of Oxford.
